# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Astilba's public site serves infrastructure-minded TypeScript developers and technical teams who are evaluating, adopting, or operating Astilba libraries. They arrive looking for a credible reason to trust the project, the shortest honest path to working code, and precise documentation of guarantees, costs, failure behavior, and release status.

## Product Purpose

Create one public identity for Astilba at `astilba.com`: the apex explains why the project exists and what is available; `/docs` explains how the released and previewed public surfaces work. Success means a technically demanding visitor can understand Astilba's position, verify its claims against public evidence, and reach the right documentation without encountering hype, hidden cloud dependency, or a roadmap presented as a product.

## Brand Personality

Exact, candid, quietly assured. The experience should feel deliberately engineered and unusually well finished, with confidence earned through clear boundaries and working details rather than volume or spectacle.

## Anti-references

- Generic venture-backed SaaS landing pages built from gradients, floating cards, inflated metrics, and interchangeable claims.
- AI-generated developer-tool aesthetics: terminal cosplay, decorative grids, glass panels, neon glow, and endless icon-card sections.
- Enterprise mega-navigation, sponsor rails, advertisements, donation blocks, or parallel product brands.
- Cloud-control-plane messaging that implies Astilba owns a customer's compute or data plane.
- Parked products, unreleased capabilities, internal research, or roadmap ideas used as public filler.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence before spectacle.** Let public code, concrete guarantees, executable examples, and explicit status language carry the argument.
2. **One identity, two questions.** The apex answers why Astilba and what is available; documentation answers how it works and what it guarantees.
3. **Boundaries are a feature.** State costs, failure behavior, preview status, and cloud limits as prominently as capabilities.
4. **Portability should be visible.** Show the same small public interface crossing runtimes and infrastructure without turning the page into a compatibility-logo wall.
5. **Restraint must still have a point of view.** Preserve Astilba's compact, border-led, nearly monochrome identity while using scale, rhythm, and the warm signal accent with enough conviction to be memorable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Preserve complete keyboard and screen-reader operation, visible focus, semantic landmarks and headings, sufficient contrast in both themes, zoom-safe and responsive layouts, and equivalent reduced-motion behavior. Treat mobile navigation, code overflow, Pagefind search, light/dark themes, and axe coverage as release gates rather than polish.
